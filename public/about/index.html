About Spiritual Compass

A free, independent guide to the world's wisdom traditions — built for seekers, not believers.

What This Site Is

Spiritual Compass is a free educational resource dedicated to exploring the world's great spiritual and wisdom traditions — Buddhism, Hinduism, Christianity, Islam, Taoism, Judaism, Norse mythology, Celtic Druidism, ancient Egyptian spirituality, Native American traditions, Greek philosophy, and more.

We believe that the spiritual heritage of humanity is too important to be locked behind academic paywalls, denominational gatekeeping, or the assumption that you already belong to one tradition. This site is for everyone who is genuinely curious — whether you are deeply devoted, quietly searching, philosophically minded, or simply open.

Our Approach

We approach every tradition with respect, intellectual honesty, and a commitment to accuracy. We do not promote one path over another. We do not ask you to believe anything. We present the teachings, practices, and history of each tradition as faithfully as we can — and trust you to draw your own conclusions.

Where traditions disagree — sometimes profoundly — we do not paper over the differences. Understanding what makes each tradition distinct is as important as recognising what they share.

⚖️

No Bias

We treat all traditions with equal seriousness and respect — ancient or modern, Eastern or Western, mainstream or esoteric.

🔍

Accuracy First

Content is researched carefully. We draw on primary texts, scholarly sources, and lived tradition — not popular myth or surface-level summaries.

🚪

Always Free

No subscriptions, no paywalls, no account required. Spiritual knowledge should be accessible to anyone willing to seek it.

🧭

You Decide

We are a compass, not a destination. We point — you walk. The path you choose is entirely your own.

What You'll Find Here

Traditions — In-depth guides to 15+ spiritual traditions, covering their history, core beliefs, gods and figures, sacred practices, and living relevance today.

Meditation — Six guided meditation practices drawn from different traditions, with an interactive timer you can use directly in your browser.

Prayer & Rituals — How people across traditions have used prayer and sacred action to connect with the divine and mark the turning points of life.

Sacred Texts — Introductions to the foundational scriptures of each tradition — what they say, how to approach them, and why they still matter.

Teachers — Profiles of the great spiritual teachers of history and the modern era who have guided millions on the inner path.

Books — A curated library of recommended reading, covering every tradition covered on this site.

Assessment — A free 15-question assessment that identifies which spiritual traditions are most aligned with your values, temperament, and what you seek.

A Note on Indigenous Traditions

We include content on Native American and other indigenous spiritual traditions with particular care. These are living traditions, not museum pieces — many of their practices remain sacred and private, and we present only what is publicly shared by indigenous communities themselves. We encourage everyone approaching indigenous wisdom to do so with humility, respect, and an awareness of the history of appropriation these traditions have endured.
