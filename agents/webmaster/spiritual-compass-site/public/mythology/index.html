The Sacred Purpose of Mythology

Mythology serves as humanity's earliest spiritual textbook. These sacred stories encode universal principles of transformation, heroism, sacrifice, and transcendence that speak to the soul across cultures and centuries.

Rather than mere entertainment, myths are initiatory narratives—maps for the journey from ordinary consciousness to divine understanding. They teach us about the eternal patterns that govern both outer world and inner psyche.

"Myths are public dreams; dreams are private myths." — Joseph Campbell
